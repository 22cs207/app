AYUSH Plants

AYUSH (Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy) represents India's traditional medicine systems. Our digital platform showcases medicinal plants used across these systems, combining ancient wisdom with modern technology to preserve and promote traditional herbal knowledge for healthcare and wellness.

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Sacred in Ayurveda, Tulsi is known as the "Queen of Herbs" with powerful adaptogenic and immune-boosting properties used across all AYUSH systems.

Ashwagandha

Known as "Indian Winter Cherry" in Ayurveda and Unani systems, this powerful adaptogen helps combat stress and enhances vitality.

Turmeric (Haldi)

A golden herb revered in Ayurveda and Siddha systems for its anti-inflammatory and healing properties, used extensively in AYUSH formulations.

Cinnamon (Dalchini)

Used in Ayurveda and Unani systems for diabetes management and digestive health, this aromatic bark is a key AYUSH medicinal plant.

Cardamom (Elaichi)

The "Queen of Spices" in Ayurveda and Siddha medicine, valued for digestive health and respiratory wellness in AYUSH treatments.

Clove (Laung)

Extensively used in Ayurveda, Unani, and Siddha systems for dental care, pain relief, and respiratory health in traditional AYUSH formulations.

General FAQ

What is the AYUSH Virtual Herbal Garden?

The AYUSH Virtual Herbal Garden is a digital knowledge platform designed to:

  • Showcase authentic medicinal plants used in Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy systems.
  • Provide interactive 3D models for better visualization of plant structures.
  • Bridge traditional wisdom with modern technology for education, research, and public awareness.
  • Encourage sustainable cultivation and conservation of medicinal flora.
What AYUSH systems are covered in this platform?

How are AYUSH plants different from regular medicinal plants?

AYUSH plants hold a distinct status compared to general medicinal flora because:

  • They are documented in classical medical texts with centuries of traditional usage.
  • Recognized officially by the Ministry of AYUSH, Government of India.
  • Backed by pharmacopoeial standards for quality, safety, and efficacy.
  • Integrated into formal healthcare systems including AYUSH hospitals and wellness centers.
  • Validated through modern scientific research while retaining cultural and traditional significance.
